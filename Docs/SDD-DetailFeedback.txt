   Expands the preliminary problem statement ~ Good
   Detailed functional and non-functional requirements ~ Good
   Constraints, and assumptions ~ Good

   The section would be more complete if it explicitly framed the system as
   a knowledge-based system rather than primarily as a game. Additional
   detail on the knowledge problem being modeled (e.g., survival reasoning
   under uncertainty) would strengthen the justification. Including a
   clearer statement of the systemís intended users and operational context
   would also improve completeness at the SDD level.

SECTION 2.2 - DETAILED KNOWLEDGE REPRESENTATION & ONTOLOGY SCOPE
   Formal representation of knowledge (rules, ontology, frames) ~ Excellent
   Entity definitions ~ Excellent
   Relationships and constraints ~ Excellent
   Diagrams ~ Only one, but it is complete

   This section is one of the strongest in the document.
   While the entities and relationships are well listed, the section would
   benefit from more explicit formalization, such as example fact templates
   or sample CLIPS fact structures. Additionally, although a diagram is
   referenced, its explanation in text is minimal; describing how the
   diagram supports reasoning flow would strengthen traceability.

SECTION 2.3 ñ INFERENCE AND REASONING STRATEGY
   Reasoning mechanisms [forward/backward chaining, hybrid] ~ Excellent
   Conflict resolution ~ appropriate to domain
   Explanation generation ~ good
   Performance optimizations ~ good

   Explanation facilities are described in an informal way that fits a game
   context but fall short of a full SDD explanation requirement. The design
   would be stronger if it explicitly stated how rule firings or state
   transitions could be traced or explained for debugging or validation.
   Including a concrete example of rule activation order or conflict
   resolution would make the reasoning strategy more verifiable.
